Privacy Policy

Last updated: 20 July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Home Call Guard collects, uses, shares and protects your personal data, and the choices and rights you have. It applies to the Home Call Guard website, registration and login, the online dashboard, and the call-screening service itself.

1.Who we are

Home Call Guard is a trading name of AFMD Ltd ("we", "us", "our"), a company registered in England and Wales under company number 07075723, with a registered office at [REGISTERED OFFICE ADDRESS TO BE CONFIRMED]. AFMD Ltd is the data controller for the personal data described in this policy. Our contact details are in Section 12 below.

2.Information we collect

Account and contact information

When you register, we collect your email address and a password (stored securely as a hash by our authentication provider, Supabase — we never store or see your password in plain text). Your account is linked to the household phone number the Service protects.

Trusted-contact telephone numbers

When you upload or add trusted contacts, we store the name and phone number you provide for each one, so that Home Call Guard can recognise their calls and let them through without screening.

Call and screening records

For each screened call, we record the caller's number, the call status, the screening result (for example, whether it was assessed as safe or a likely scam), a decision reason, and technical details such as processing time and which detection method was used. Where our AI classification step is used, the caller's spoken words are converted to text and analysed at the time of the call to help classify it — this text is not stored in our database once the call has been processed.

Payment information

We do not collect or store your card details. Payment is handled entirely by our payment processor, Stripe — see Section 4. We store only your Stripe customer and subscription identifiers, and your subscription status.

Technical and session information

We use strictly necessary cookies to keep you securely logged in (see Section 9). We do not currently use analytics, advertising or tracking cookies.

4.Who we share your information with

We share personal data only with service providers who help us run Home Call Guard, under contracts that require them to protect it appropriately. We do not sell your personal data.

  • Stripe (payment processing) — handles your payment details and processes your subscription charges directly; we never see or store your full card details.
  • Supabase (database and authentication) — stores your account and household data, and manages secure login. Hosted in the UK (London, eu-west-2 region).
  • Railway (application hosting) — runs the Home Call Guard application. Hosted in the UK (London, eu-west-2 region).
  • Twilio (telephony) — routes and connects calls to and through the Service so calls can be screened.
  • OpenAI (AI call classification) — receives a text version of an unknown caller's spoken words, at the moment of the call, solely to help classify whether the call is likely to be a scam.

We may also disclose personal data where required by law, to protect our rights, or in connection with a business transfer (such as a merger or acquisition), in which case we will take reasonable steps to ensure your data continues to be protected.

5.International data transfers

Some of our service providers (including Stripe, Twilio and OpenAI) are based outside the UK, or may process data outside the UK/European Economic Area as part of providing their service to us. Where this happens, we rely on appropriate safeguards recognised under UK data protection law — such as Standard Contractual Clauses, an equivalent UK-approved mechanism, or the provider's own adequacy/compliance certifications — to ensure your data remains protected to a UK standard.

6.How long we keep your information

We keep personal data for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes it was collected for, and to meet legal, accounting or reporting requirements. As a general guide:

  • Account and trusted-contact data: for as long as you have an active account, and for a limited period afterwards in case you wish to reactivate or raise a query.
  • Call and screening records: for a limited retention period to let you review recent activity and for service-quality purposes, after which they are deleted or anonymised.
  • Billing records: for as long as required by UK tax and accounting law (typically several years) after your subscription ends.

7.Automated screening decisions

Home Call Guard uses automated methods (keyword analysis and AI classification) to assess incoming calls from numbers not on your trusted-contact list. This affects how a call is handled, but does not produce any legal or similarly significant automated decision about you as our customer. If you believe a call was screened incorrectly, you can contact us and we will look into it.

8.How we protect your information

We use industry-standard security measures, including encrypted (HTTPS) connections, securely hashed passwords, and access controls that restrict who and what can read or modify your data. No system can be guaranteed 100% secure, but we work to keep your information protected and to respond quickly to any issue.

9.Cookies and session storage

We use two strictly necessary cookies to keep you securely signed in: an access token and a refresh token. Both are set as httpOnly cookies (not readable by page scripts), transmitted only over secure connections in production, and used solely to recognise your logged-in session. We do not use these, or any other cookies, for advertising, analytics or cross-site tracking.

10.Your rights under UK GDPR

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you;
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete data;
  • Request deletion of your data, in certain circumstances;
  • Restrict or object to certain processing;
  • Receive a copy of your data in a portable format, where technically feasible;
  • Withdraw consent at any time, where processing is based on consent.

To exercise any of these rights, email support@homecallguard.co.uk. We will respond without undue delay, and in any event within one month, as required by UK GDPR. We may need to verify your identity before actioning certain requests.
